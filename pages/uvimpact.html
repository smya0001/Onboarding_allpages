Data about UV impacts

The sun's ultraviolet (UV) radiation is classified into three categories according to wavelength: UVA, UVB, and UVC. UVA radiation possesses the greatest wavelength and is linked to the process of skin ageing, whereas UVB radiation possesses a shorter wavelength and is related with skin burning. The Earth's ozone layer predominantly absorbs UVC radiation, preventing it from reaching the Earth's surface. Extended exposure to UVA and UVB radiation can result in Multiple dermatological conditions, such as sunburn, premature skin ageing, and perhaps skin cancer. It is crucial to shield your skin from UV rays throughout the year, not only in the summer or when you are at the beach.
